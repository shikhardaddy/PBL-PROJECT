Addressing Urban Congestion

The Challenge of Static Traffic Management

Traffic congestion remains a persistent and costly challenge in urban environments, leading to increased fuel consumption, severe air pollution, and significant time wastage for commuters. Traditional traffic signals operate on fixed timing cycles, a static mechanism that fails to adapt to dynamic traffic density changes.

  • • Long waiting times during low-traffic periods.
  • • Poor traffic management during peak hours.
  • • Wastage of manpower and energy.
  • • Inability to consider weather, events, or holidays.

The proposed solution is a predictive, data-driven, semi-automated system designed to intelligently optimize signal timing.

Core Goals of the Intelligent Signal System

Predict Traffic Patterns

Utilize historical and external datasets to forecast traffic density across different times and conditions.

Analyze Mass Behavior

Incorporate office hours, festivals, tourist seasons, and large-scale behavioral patterns.

Integrate Diverse Data

Combine Google Maps, Weather APIs, and Government traffic databases.

Minimize Power Use

Deploy limited IoT sensors strictly for real-time validation.

Enable Dynamic Adjustment

Adjust signal timing dynamically based on AI predictions.

Two-Layer Hybrid Architecture

Layer 1: Predictive AI Engine

This layer forecasts future traffic using LSTM, Regression, and Random Forest models trained on historical datasets. It integrates:
  • • Google Maps Platform API
  • • Weather API (OpenWeather)
  • • Government traffic databases

Layer 2: Sensor-Assisted Validation

Low-power IoT sensors (IR/Ultrasonic) validate AI predictions. If deviation exceeds threshold, corrective adjustments are triggered while maintaining energy efficiency.
